A couple of weeks ago, I presented my thesis titled
“A Categorical View of the Jordan–Hölder–Schreier Theorem for Modules.”

In this work, I proposed a categorical generalization of this classic result from module theory and abstract algebra. I introduced the definition of a category over a module and a Jordan category, capturing the precise structure needed for the theorem to hold in a categorical framework.

This thesis also marks a personal milestone—it was the final project of my undergraduate degree in Applied Mathematics at the Universidad Autónoma de Aguascalientes.

I’m incredibly grateful for these five years: for the friends, the professors, and the opportunity to contribute—however modestly—to mathematics. Here's to what comes next!
